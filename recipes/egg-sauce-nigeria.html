Egg Sauce (Nigerian Style)

Description

Egg sauce is a common breakfast in Nigeria. Usually paired with a carb source (e.g. yam, plantain, potatoes). It is similar to scrambled eggs but done with a variety of vegetables. Note: The recipe below is very basic egg sauce, you can always add more vegetables or other sources of protein to it.

Ingredients

  • Eggs
  • Onion
  • Garlic
  • Tomatoes
  • Red Bell Pepper
  • Scotch Bonnet Pepper
  • Curry Powder
  • Smoked paprika
  • Salt
  • Oil

Steps

  1. Chop the garlic finely.
  2. Chop the tomatoes, peppers and onion. Or blend coarsely in a food processor - If you are doing this, blend the onion first and separate it from the other ingredients.
  3. Break the eggs and whisk with a pinch of salt, and keep aside.
  4. Heat the oil in a non-stick frying pan, then add onions. Cook until the onions are soft and starting to go brown.
  5. Add the garlic, curry powder and smoked paprika, stir for 1 min, then add the peppers and tomatoes. Mix the content of the pan well and leave to simmer.
  6. Check the sauce and occasionally stir until it is reduced. This should take at least 10 mins, by which time the oil would be separated from the sauce.
